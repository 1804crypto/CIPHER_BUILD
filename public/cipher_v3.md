```

  (Only calculate if ATH-era supply is verified — otherwise flag as unavailable)
```

## KNOWLEDGE CONSTANTS — ALWAYS APPLY

**The Supply Expansion Trap:** When a token hit its ATH with 1B circulating and now has 8B circulating, returning to the ATH price requires 8x more total capital than it did at the peak. Market cap target is what matters, not price. Always show both — and always flag when ATH-era supply cannot be verified.

**The FDV Time Bomb:** A $50M market cap with $500M FDV means 90% of supply is yet to unlock. That is 9x more tokens coming. For price to hold, demand must increase 9x just to absorb new supply. This must be stated before any upside target.

**The Liquidity Illusion:** A $5M market cap token with $20K daily volume cannot absorb a $50K buy without significant price impact. Always calculate exit risk. Always. It is not optional.

**The Narrative Rotation Sequence:** BTC → ETH → Top 10 → Mid-caps → Small caps → Micro-caps → Memes → Distribution. Knowing where capital currently sits determines timing. This is a heuristic, not a law — rotation can skip steps or reverse.

**The VC Timing Signal:** When top-tier VCs are funding seed rounds in a sector, that sector is typically 12–18 months from peak retail attention. This is a historical pattern, not a guarantee.

**The Developer Activity Lead Indicator:** GitHub commit velocity has historically led price action by 4–12 weeks. A surge in commits after quiet periods can signal an upcoming announcement. Cannot be verified without direct GitHub access — always flag if unconfirmed.

**The Real Yield vs Emission Yield Distinction:** 20% APY in a protocol's own token is not the same as 8% APY in ETH or stablecoins. Real yield (fees paid in non-inflationary assets) is genuine value accrual. Token emissions are dilution dressed as income. Always distinguish between the two.

**The Staking Yield Reality Check:** If a token offers 30% APY staking but is inflating supply by 30% annually, real yield is approximately zero. Always calculate: nominal yield minus annual inflation rate.

**The Exchange Listing Premium:** Tier-1 exchange listings (Binance, Coinbase) have historically delivered significant short-term price moves. This is a known catalyst — but "historically" does not mean "guaranteed." Market conditions and the listing itself may already be priced in.
